Waarom Ik TRT Zie Als Medisch Behandeling

Let me be clear: I don't view TRT as een shortcut, enhancement, or levensstijl choice. I see it as medical intervention voor een medical condition - hypogonadism or suboptimal testosterone production.

Het Medisch Model

  • Diagnosis First: TRT should only follow proper medical evaluation
  • Symptom-Based: Behandeling of actual symptoms affecting quality of life
  • Conservative Dosing: Restore normal levels, not exceed them
  • Ongoing Monitoring: Regular medical supervision en blood work
  • Health Focus: Goal is health en well-being, not prestaties

Not Enhancement

TRT is fundamentally different from enhancement/doping:

  • TRT Goal: Restore normal physiological function
  • Enhancement Goal: Exceed normal human limits
  • TRT Dosing: 100-200mg per week
  • Enhancement Dosing: 300-1000mg+ per week
  • TRT Monitoring: Health-focused blood work
  • Enhancement Monitoring: Performance-focused metrics

Symptoms That Matter

Numbers alone aren't enough. I look for these quality-of-life impacts:

  • Recovery: Poor recovery from exercise despite good programming
  • Mental Health: Mood issues, depression, lack of motivation
  • Sexual Function: Decreased libido, prestaties issues
  • Body Composition: Inability to maintain muscle/lose fat despite proper diet/training
  • Sleep: Poor sleep quality affecting daily function

Common TRT Misconceptions I Want to Correct

Misconception 1: "TRT is a fountain of youth"

Reality: TRT restores normal function; it doesn't make je superhuman. Je zult feel better if je testosterone was low, maar it's not magic.

Misconception 2: "TRT will solve all my problems"

Reality: TRT can improve energy, mood, en motivation, maar het won't fix poor habits, relationship issues, or character flaws.

Misconception 3: "TRT is dangerous"

Reality: When properly monitored en dosed, TRT is generally very safe. Most risks are overblown or related to improper use.

Misconception 4: "TRT is easy"

Reality: TRT requires ongoing commitment, monitoring, injection skills, potential side effect management, en levensstijl maintenance.

Misconception 5: "Once je start, je can never stop"

Reality: While TRT is typically long-term, men can stop with proper protocols. Natural production may return, though often not to previous levels.

Practical TRT Considerations

Choosing a Doctor

Not all doctors are equal when it comes to TRT. Look for:

  • Hormone expertise: Understands male hormone optimalisatie
  • Comprehensive approach: Looks at levensstijl factors too
  • Conservative dosing: Doesn't start with high doses
  • Regular monitoring: Quarterly blood work initially
  • Open communication: Listens to concerns en adjusts accordingly

TRT Protocol Design

My preferred approach for TRT:

  • Start low: 100-120mg testosterone per week
  • Frequent injections: Every 3.5 days (twice weekly)
  • Monitor closely: Blood work at 6, 12, 24 weeks
  • Adjust gradually: Small dose changes based on response
  • Lifestyle continuation: Maintain all optimalisatie practices

Long-term Perspectief

TRT is typically een lifelong commitment. Consider:

  • Injection routine: Twice weekly for years/decades
  • Medisch monitoring: Regular blood work en check-ups
  • Cost: $100-300 per month including monitoring
  • Travel considerations: Managing treatment while traveling
  • Potential complications: Side effect management
