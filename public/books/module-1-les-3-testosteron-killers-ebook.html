Category 1: Endocrine Disrupting Chemicals (EDCs)

Plastic-Based Killers

BPA (Bisphenol A) & BPS:

  • Sources: Plastic bottles, food containers, receipts, canned foods
  • Mechanism: Mimics estrogen, directly lowers testosterone
  • Impact: 10-20% testosterone reduction with regular exposure
  • Hidden sources: Thermal receipts, dental sealings, some water bottles

Phthalates:

  • Sources: Fragrances, lotions, shampoos, vinyl, plastic wrap
  • Mechanism: Disrupts testosterone synthesis at cellular level
  • Impact: 15-25% reduction in free testosterone
  • Accumulation: Builds up in body fat over time

Personal Care Product Killers

Parabens:

  • Sources: Shampoos, deodorants, lotions, cosmetics
  • Mechanism: Estrogen-like activity, testosterone suppression
  • Detection: Look for methylparaben, propylparaben, etc.

Triclosan:

  • Sources: Antibacterial soaps, toothpaste, hand sanitizers
  • Mechanism: Thyroid disruption, indirect testosterone impact
  • Bonus damage: Destroys beneficial gut bacteria

Category 2: Dietary Testosteron Destroyers

Soy & Phytoestrogens

High-Risk Soy Products:

  • Soy protein isolate: Concentrated estrogen-like compounds
  • Processed soy: Soy milk, tofu, tempeh, edamame
  • Hidden soy: Lecithin, vegetable protein, many processed foods
  • Mechanism: Isoflavones mimic estrogen, suppress testosterone

Other Phytoestrogen Sources:

  • Flax seeds: Extremely high in lignans
  • Hops (beer): One of strongest phytoestrogens
  • Licorice root: Direct testosterone synthesis inhibition

Processed Food Killers

Vegetable & Seed Oils:

  • Sources: Canola, soybean, sunflower, safflower, corn oil
  • Mechanism: High omega-6, inflammatory, SHBG increase
  • Ubiquity: In 95% of restaurant food, packaged foods
  • Alternative: Coconut oil, olive oil, animal fats

High Sugar & Refined Carbs:

  • Mechanism: Insulin spikes suppress testosterone production
  • Chronic impact: Insulin resistance = chronic low testosterone
  • Sources: Bread, pasta, candy, sodas, processed snacks
