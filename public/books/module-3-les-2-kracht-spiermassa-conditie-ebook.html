Key Strength Movements

The Big 4 Compound Movements:

1. Deadlift - The King of Strength

  • Muscle Groups: Posterior chain dominance - glutes, hamstrings, back
  • Functional Transfer: Picking up objects, posture improvement

Types van Cardiovascular Training

1. HIIT (High-Intensity Interval Training)

  • Protocol: Short bursts van high intensiteit with rest periods
  • Benefits: Time-efficient, preserves muscle mass
  • Examples: Sprint intervals, bike intervals, rowing intervals
  • Frequency: 2-3x per week, 15-20 minutes
  • Intensity: 85-95% max heart rate during work periods

2. LISS (Low-Intensity Steady State)

  • Protocol: Sustained moderate intensiteit effort
  • Benefits: Active recovery, fat burning, aerobic base
  • Examples: Brisk walking, light jogging, cycling
  • Frequency: 3-5x per week, 30-45 minutes
  • Intensity: 60-70% max heart rate

3. Circuit Training

  • Protocol: Resistance exercises with minimal rest
  • Benefits: Combines strength en conditioning
  • Examples: Kettlebell circuits, bodyweight circuits
  • Frequency: 2-3x per week
  • Duration: 20-30 minutes

Oplossing: Focus op consistentie, niet op perfectie. 80% consistent is beter dan 100% perfect maar onregelmatig.

Oplossing: Vind een accountability partner of groep die je fysieke prestaties ondersteunt. Omring jezelf met mensen die dezelfde doelen hebben.

Obstakel 5: Verleidingen

Oplossing: Verwijder verleidingen uit je omgeving. Maak fysieke prestaties makkelijker en slechte keuzes moeilijker.

1. Strength Development Protocol

Programming Essentials:

  • Frequency: 3-4 strength sessions per week
  • Rep Ranges: 1-6 reps voor max strength

  • Progression: Add weight weekly (2.5-5 lbs)

Hypertrophy Essentials:

  • Frequency: Each muscle group 2-3x per week
  • Rep Ranges: 6-15 reps voor muscle growth
  • Rest Periods: 60-90 seconds between sets
  • Volume: 10-20 sets per muscle group per week
  • Progression: Add reps, then weight

Weekly Split Example:

  • Day 1: Chest, Shoulders, Triceps
  • Day 2: Back, Biceps
  • Day 3: Legs (Quads, Glutes, Calves)
  • Day 4: Rest or Light Cardio
  • Day 5: Upper Body (Compound focus)
  • Day 6: Legs (Hamstrings, Glutes)
3. Conditioning Protocol

HIIT Session Structure:

  • Warm-up: 5 minutes easy pace
  • Work Period: 30 seconds all-out effort
  • Rest Period: 90 seconds easy recovery
  • Repeat: 8-12 intervals
  • Cool-down: 5 minutes easy pace

LISS Guidelines:

  • Target Heart Rate: 120-140 BPM
  • Duration: 30-45 minutes
  • Perceived Exertion: 6-7 out of 10
  • Frequency: 3-5x per week
  • Activities: Walking, cycling, swimming
